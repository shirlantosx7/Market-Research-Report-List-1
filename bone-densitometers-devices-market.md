Axial bone densitometers primarily focus on measuring bone density in the spine and hip, providing detailed assessments for osteoporosis diagnosis and management. Peripheral bone densitometers, on the other hand, are used for assessing bone density in the peripheral regions such as the wrist or heel, offering a portable and cost-effective option for screening.
