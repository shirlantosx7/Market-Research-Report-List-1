<p><p>AC Adjustable Speed Drives (ASDs) are categorized into three main types based on voltage levels: Low Voltage Drives (up to 1 kV), Medium Voltage Drives (1 kV to 7.2 kV), and High Voltage Drives (above 7.2 kV). Low Voltage Drives are commonly used in residential and industrial applications for motors below 1000 HP. Medium Voltage Drives cater to larger industrial processes, offering enhanced efficiency. High Voltage Drives, utilized in large-scale operations, are essential for high-capacity motors, providing superior control and energy savings.</p></p>
