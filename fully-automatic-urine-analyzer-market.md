<p><p>The fully automatic urine analyzer market is segmented primarily into two types: microscopy methods and chemistry methods. Microscopy methods analyze urine samples using automated imaging and particle detection techniques to identify cellular components and microorganisms. In contrast, chemistry methods rely on biochemical reactions and sensors to assess the composition of urine, measuring parameters like pH, density, and the presence of proteins or glucose. Both methods enhance diagnostic accuracy and efficiency in clinical laboratories, supporting timely patient care and decision-making.</p></p>
