<p><p>A bimetal thermostat is a temperature control device that utilizes two different types of metal joined together to form a bimetallic strip. As temperatures fluctuate, the differing expansion rates of the metals cause the strip to bend, enabling it to open or close an electrical circuit. Bimetal thermostats are widely used in various applications, including household appliances, HVAC systems, and industrial equipment, due to their reliability and cost-effectiveness.</p><p>The bimetal thermostat market is witnessing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for energy-efficient heating and cooling systems.
