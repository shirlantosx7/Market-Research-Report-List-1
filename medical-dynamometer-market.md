Electronic medical dynamometers use sensors and digital displays to measure muscle strength and provide accurate, real-time data, enhancing precision in assessments. In contrast, mechanical medical dynamometers rely on traditional springs or levers for strength measurement, often being more straightforward but less precise. Both types serve vital roles in rehabilitation, physical therapy, and strength assessment, catering to diverse clinical and research needs.</p></p>
