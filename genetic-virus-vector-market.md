<p><p>The genetic virus vector market comprises several types, each serving distinct purposes in gene therapy and research. Retroviral vectors are efficient in integrating genes into host genomes, making them suitable for stable gene expression. Adenoviral vectors are known for high transduction efficiency and can carry larger genetic payloads, but they do not integrate into the host genome. Adeno-associated viral vectors offer low immunogenicity and long-term expression, while the “Other” category includes unique or specialized vectors catering to specific therapeutic needs.</p></p>
